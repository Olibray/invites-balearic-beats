The Eclipse

Sunset and totality,
at the same moment.

On 12 August, totality reaches Mallorca at 8:31pm — 94 seconds of complete darkness with the sun sitting low on the western horizon. The partial eclipse begins at 7:38pm, giving you nearly an hour of the sky shifting before the moment arrives. The rooftop faces west over the Mediterranean, putting the eclipse exactly in frame. We built the night around this.

7:38pm — partial eclipse begins, sun starts to dim
8:31pm — totality begins, darkness falls over the sea
8:31:32pm — peak totality · 94 seconds · sun on the horizon
Rooftop faces west — eclipse is directly in front of you over the water

Where to Stay

Port de Pollença is
five minutes away.

Cala Sant Vicenç sits on Mallorca's north coast, a short drive from Port de Pollença. Stay on the bay, explore the town in the morning, and drive over for sunset.

Recommended · Next Door

Club Del Sol

The nearest hotel to the villa — a short walk from the rooftop. Our recommended base if you want to be as close to the event as possible.

Also Consider

Anywhere in Port de Pollença

The port is five minutes by car. Hotels, apartments, and rentals line the bay. A relaxed base with good restaurants and the long Passeig Saralegui to walk off the night.
